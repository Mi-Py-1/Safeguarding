The Safeguarding Guide

Everyone deserves a right to safety and freedom from abuse, persecution and exploitation. The Safeguarding Guide is dedicated to supporting our communities through the training, outreach and information provision we offer on this site.


We believe that by providing these resources to members of the community, as well as professionals, we are most able to reduce abuse.


Thank you for visiting this website. This means you are either a professional or student who wants more information or are a member of the public concerned about the welfare of someone. Either way, you care, so thank you.

Adult Safeguarding

Adult safeguarding is the protection of an adult's right to live free from abuse and neglect, and to promote their wellbeing. It involves a collaborative effort between people and organizations to prevent and stop abuse or neglect, and to ensure the safety and wellbeing of adults.

The six principles of safeguarding adults are: Empowerment, Prevention, Proportionality, Protection, Partnership, and Accountability


Child Safeguarding

Child safeguarding is the process of protecting children from harm, abuse, or neglect, and ensuring their health and development are not impaired. It also involves providing support to children when problems arise, and ensuring they grow up in a safe and effective environment.

If you think a child is in immediate danger, you should call the police on 999.
